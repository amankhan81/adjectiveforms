What are Adjectives?

Definition

Adjectives are Data Modifiers. They attach to nouns to define quality, quantity, or identity.

They answer: Which? What kind? How many?

Syntax Examples

  • The neon city glowed.
  • We have multiple targets.
  • That is a massive glitch.

The Comparative

Usage: 1 vs 1

Use this to compare exactly TWO entities.

Rule: Adjective + "-er"
OR "More" + Adjective.

Example: "The laser is faster than the bullet."

Created by: Amanullah Khan

The Superlative

Usage: The Absolute Best

Use for THREE OR MORE items to show the limit of a quality.

Rule: The + Adjective + "-est"
OR "Most" + Adjective.

Example: "This is the most advanced AI in the world."

Created by: Amanullah Khan

Spelling Protocols

Fast Track

Short words (1 syllable): Add -er / -est.

Cool → Cooler → Coolest

The Y-Swap

Ends in 'Y'? Delete 'Y', add -ier / -iest.

Easy → Easier → Easiest

Long Range

2+ Syllables? Use More / Most.

Famous → More Famous
