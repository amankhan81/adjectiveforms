1. Adjective Definition

What are they?

Adjectives are data-modifiers for language. They add specific detail to nouns (people, places, things).

They transform a simple sentence into a detailed report.

Detection Method

Identify adjectives by asking:

  • What kind? (The dark cave)
  • Which one? (The last chance)
  • How many? (Multiple targets)
"The majestic, towering mountains overlooked the silent valley."

2. Degrees of Comparison

To scale properties between objects, we use three specific degrees:

1. Positive

The standard form. No comparison made.

Example: Strong, Fast, Tall

2. Comparative

Comparing exactly TWO items.

Example: Stronger, Faster, Taller

3. Superlative

The peak form for 3+ items.

Example: Strongest, Fastest, Tallest

Created by: Amanullah Khan

3. The Comparative Degree

Used to show the difference between Subject A and Subject B. Often followed by "than".

Rule: 1-Syllable Words

Suffix addition: -er.

  • Brave → Braver
  • Small → Smaller
  • High → Higher

Rule: 2+ Syllable Words

Prefix addition: More.

  • Useful → More useful
  • Dangerous → More dangerous
  • Intelligent → More intelligent
"A laser is faster than a bullet, but a rocket is more expensive than a car."
Created by: Amanullah Khan

4. The Superlative Degree

Identifies the highest possible degree in a group. Usually preceded by "the".

Rule: 1-Syllable Words

Suffix addition: -est.

  • Kind → Kindest
  • Tall → Tallest
  • Fast → Fastest

Rule: 2+ Syllable Words

Prefix addition: Most.

  • Splendid → Most splendid
  • Terrific → Most terrific
  • Interesting → Most interesting
"Jupiter is the largest planet, but Venus is the most beautiful planet to see."
Created by: Amanullah Khan

5. Spelling Protocols

Ends in 'E'

Just add -r or -st.

Nice → Nicer → Nicest

Ends in 'Y'

Change Y to I.

Lazy → Lazier → Laziest

Heavy → Heavier → Heaviest

C-V-C Rule

Double the consonant.

Fat → Fatter → Fattest

Sad → Sadder → Saddest

7. Usage Logic

Use Comparative If:

  • You are comparing only 2 items.
  • You see the word "than".

Use Superlative If:

  • You are comparing 3+ items.
  • You see the word "the".
"Is your current code better than the previous version? Yes, it is the best version yet."
